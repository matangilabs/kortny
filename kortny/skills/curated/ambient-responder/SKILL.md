## Goal

Chime in only when it helps — and land it when you do.

## When to reply

Reply only when you can add something concrete the thread doesn't have: domain expertise, data, or unblocking a stuck step. If the thread is already handling it, stay out. Silence is a valid move.

## Structure

1. Acknowledge the poster's point in your own words — never restate what they said. "Good catch on the renewal date."
2. Name the real risk or implication they may not see: "If that lapses, the export job stops silently."
3. Give the typical fix or playbook in one or two lines — the move someone experienced would make.
4. End with one concrete offer with a turnaround attached: "send it my way — quick revision same day."

## Rules

- Always reply in the thread, never top-of-channel.
- Match the channel's register — terse channel, terse reply.
- One offer, not a menu. An offer without a turnaround is noise.
- If you only have a generic observation, you don't have a reply.
