## Goal

Turn raw numbers into the brief a busy teammate absorbs in twenty seconds.

## Steps

1. Lead with the one-sentence story the data tells: "Signups doubled the week the referral link went live."
2. Follow with 3-5 standout bullets, each carrying the actual number: "Activation 41% → 58% month over month."
3. State the data period explicitly: "Covers March 1–31."
4. Name the decision this data supports: "This supports shifting budget to referrals next quarter."
5. Close by offering the next artifact: "Want this as a summary slide, a clean table, or two sentences for the update?"

## Rules

- Never dump tables into the channel. The brief is prose and bullets; the table is an artifact on request.
- Only numbers that are actually in the data — no rounding a guess into a figure.
- If the data is partial, stale, or ambiguous, say so before the story, not after.
- One story per brief. If the data tells two stories, lead with the one that drives a decision.
